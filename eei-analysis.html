Executive Summary

The Ethical Ecosystem Index (EEI) measures the divergence between technological capability and ethical governance across six critical domains. Our analysis projects a widening structural gap between 2026 and 2033, with several domains approaching irreversible tipping points.

Key Finding: The rate of ethical divergence is accelerating faster than institutional capacity to respond. By 2028, three of six domains are projected to cross critical thresholds without significant policy intervention.

This is not a prediction of doom. It is a diagnostic instrument. The EEI Predictor allows users to model intervention scenarios and assess the probability space of different policy choices. The point is not to paralyze—it is to arm decision-makers with clarity.

Cross-Domain Cascade Analysis

The most dangerous aspect of the current trajectory is not any single domain's deterioration, but the cascade effects between domains. When one domain crosses a critical threshold, it amplifies pressure on connected domains.

3.1 Primary Cascade Vectors
CASCADE MECHANISM RISK LEVEL
Climate → Economy Extreme weather disrupts agriculture, infrastructure. Insurance markets stress. Commodity spikes. CRITICAL
Geopolitics → Economy Sanctions, trade wars, supply chain weaponization. Energy security becomes leverage. CRITICAL
Technology → Epistemic AI-generated content floods information channels. Trust in all media collapses. HIGH
Economy → Social Job displacement + inflation = social unrest. Mental health crisis amplifies. HIGH
Climate → Geopolitics Resource scarcity drives territorial conflicts. Climate migration triggers border crises. HIGH
Epistemic → Social Polarization makes collective action impossible. Conspiracy thinking normalizes. ELEVATED

Key insight: The cascade between Climate and Economy has the highest potential for irreversible damage because both domains have physical tipping points. Unlike political or epistemic shifts, environmental and economic collapses cannot be easily reversed with policy changes.

Methodology

The EEI is a composite index built from publicly available data sources, weighted by domain impact and cascade multipliers. It is designed as a cognitive tool, not a prediction engine.

5.1 Data Sources
  • Geopolitics: SIPRI, ACLED, ICG conflict trackers
  • Economy: IMF, World Bank, BIS, national central banks
  • Climate: IPCC, NASA GISS, NOAA, Copernicus
  • Technology: AI Index (Stanford), CSET, ITU, EFF
  • Social: WHO, ILO, UN HDI, Gallup World Poll
  • Epistemic: Reuters Institute, V-Dem, Freedom House, MIT Media Lab
5.2 Limitations
  • Black swan events are inherently unmodelable. The EEI projects trends, not events.
  • Cascade multipliers are estimated from historical analogies (limited sample size).
  • Domain boundaries are heuristic, not ontological. Reality doesn't respect our categories.
  • The index is intentionally pessimistic in its baseline. This is by design: it is better to be wrong about danger than wrong about safety.
